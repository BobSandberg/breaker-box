# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is the homeowner maintaining a detailed map of a specific house electrical system. They use the app on a phone while standing in rooms, at outlets, at the breaker panel, or near equipment, and on a computer when entering or reviewing larger amounts of data.

They are trying to identify which circuit controls an electrical point, record verified mappings, understand uncertainty, and reason about known or possible loads before changing or using a circuit.

## Product Purpose

Breaker Box exists to replace panel-door labels and spreadsheet drift with a consistent relational map of panels, breaker positions, circuits, rooms, electrical points, verification history, and advisory load planning.

Success means the user can quickly answer: what controls this point, how confident is that mapping, what loads may be on this circuit, and what still needs verification.

The app is advisory only. It must never imply that electrical work is safe without independent physical verification that the line is de-energized.

## Brand Personality

Competent and precise. The interface should feel like a trustworthy workshop instrument, not a consumer smart-home toy or a generic SaaS dashboard.

The product can be terse and technical where accuracy matters, but it should not require electrician-only jargon to navigate ordinary household tasks.

## Anti-references

- Spreadsheet dressed as an app.
- Generic SaaS dashboard with interchangeable cards.
- Electrician-only pro tool overloaded with dense jargon.
- Dark neon hacker panel.
- Minimal interface that hides important uncertainty.
- Decorative status color without text or shape support.

## Design Principles

1. **Expose uncertainty.** Unknown breaker assignments, unknown wire gauge, unknown wattage, and unverified mappings must be visible and actionable.
2. **Start from the room.** The fastest path should support standing in a physical space and finding the relevant electrical point.
3. **Separate physical reality from labels.** Panels, breaker positions, circuits, points, and loads are distinct concepts, and the UI should preserve that separation without making it burdensome.
4. **Make verification ordinary.** Confirming, contradicting, or annotating a mapping should be a normal part of use, not a hidden admin task.
5. **Prefer precise guidance over polished vagueness.** Copy and layout should help the user make safe, concrete decisions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Status must never rely on color alone.
- Primary experience will be desktop, but mobile will follow after
- Mobile tap targets should be large enough for one-handed use.
- Primary flows should work well on a phone while walking around the house.
